About Us

Our names are Eesha Rao Agrahar and Roschelle D’souza and we are juniors at Tompkins High School in Katy, Texas. We are part of a club called Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA), and as a part of this club, are participating in the Community Service Project.

Kendleton is a food desert due to the fact that the nearest grocery store is 17 miles away. Of the food deserts in Fort Bend County, Kendleton is in need of special attention due to its population largely consisting of senior citizens and low-income families. To further explain, the average American makes two trips to the grocery store per week; however, since over 60% of the residents of Kendleton are senior citizens, many residents have limited mobility and do not have the privilege to travel so often, and the ones that do, lack the money to keep up with rising food and gas prices.

Our mission is to provide the residents of Kendleton with the attention they deserve by providing this town with monthly food kits, setting up a successful community garden, and petitioning to bring a grocery store to the city.

Our project aims to provide the residents of Kendleton with life’s most basic necessity: food. Greater than this, we want to raise awareness of this situation in the whole country so that all Americans have the resources they need to live happy, healthy lives.

So what exactly is a food desert?

By definition, a food desert is an urban area where it is difficult for residents to have access to fresh produce. In the United States, 23.5 million people live in a food desert, and in the city of Houston, the 8th richest city in the world, 500,000 people live in a designated food desert.

Kendleton makes up nearly 10% of the total Fort Bend County food desert population.
